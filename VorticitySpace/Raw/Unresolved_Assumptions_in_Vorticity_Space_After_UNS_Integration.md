## 3. Remaining Unresolved Assumptions

While UNS provides formal completeness, several **interpretive** and **ontological** assumptions from the Vorticity model remain open.

### **A. Ontological Substrate — The Nature of Motion and Rotation**

Vorticity Space began with the intuition that **reality is fundamentally vortical**—that structures of flow and rotation generate differentiation and stability.  
UNS explains *why* differentiation must occur, but it does not specify *why* this should appear as **rotation or vorticity**.

**Unresolved Question:**  
Is “vorticity” literal (dynamical motion in a continuous substrate) or metaphorical (phase circulation within relational value-space)?

**Possible Directions:**  
- Interpret vorticity as the **geometric curvature** of normalized value distributions.  
- Treat rotational behavior as emergent phase structure rather than fundamental motion.

---

### **B. Metric Emergence — The Appearance of Dimensions**

Vorticity Space suggests that **space and time** arise from structured flow, while UNS derives asymmetry as necessary for definability.  
What remains missing is an account of **dimensional selection**—why certain relational degrees of freedom stabilize as the familiar 3+1 spacetime metric.

**Unresolved Question:**  
What determines the specific number and nature of emergent dimensions?

**Possible Direction:**  
Derive dimensionality as a property of **differential stability** within normalized relational systems.

### **C. Ontological Realism — The Status of the “Field”**

The original Vorticity hypothesis assumes an underlying continuous **field** in which vortices arise.  
UNS, however, describes reality as pure relational value-space; no background substrate is necessary.

**Unresolved Question:**  
Is there an ontological “field” beneath UNS, or is the field simply the perceptual surface of relational differentiation?

**Possible Direction:**  
Reframe “the field” as an emergent texture of relational unity—phenomenological, not fundamental.

Vorticity Space was expressed dynamically, using concepts of flow, momentum, and energy.

### **E. The Observer Problem — Partitioning Within Unity**

In Vorticity Space, observers are **localized vortices of coherence**.

**Unresolved Question:**
